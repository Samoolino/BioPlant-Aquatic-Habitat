# Milestone 1 — Bill of Materials (BOM)

## Target Budget Range
Low-cost prototype: $150 – $500 equivalent

## Core Components

### 1. Chamber
- Acrylic or plastic tank (10–20 L)
- Estimated cost: $20–$60

### 2. Pump
- Small water pump or peristaltic pump
- Estimated cost: $15–$80

### 3. Tubing & Fittings
- PVC tubing
- Hose clamps
- Connectors
- Estimated cost: $10–$40

### 4. Membrane Materials
- Silicone sheet OR PTFE membrane OR microporous filter sheet
- Estimated cost: $20–$100

### 5. Gas Input
Option A:
- Aquarium air pump ($10–$30)

Option B:
- Oxygen cylinder + regulator (if available)

### 6. Measurement
- Dissolved oxygen meter
- Estimated cost: $50–$200

- Thermometer or digital probe
- Estimated cost: $5–$20

### 7. Structure & Assembly
- PVC frame or acrylic sheet
- Sealant (silicone)
- Screws, fasteners
- Estimated cost: $10–$50

## Optional Upgrades
- Flow meter ($20–$80)
- Data logger ($30–$150)
- pH sensor ($15–$60)

## Nigeria Sourcing Notes
- Aquarium shops → pumps, tubing, DO meters
- Industrial markets → PVC, valves, connectors
- Medical supply distributors → oxygen regulators
- Online (Jumia/Amazon) → sensors and electronics

## Budget Strategy
Start with:
- air pump
- basic membrane
- manual data logging

Upgrade only after first successful dataset.
