# Experimental Milestone 1 — Oxygen Transfer Validation

## Objective
Demonstrate that a controlled membrane system can measurably increase oxygen transfer into a fluid compared to passive diffusion.

## Why this matters
This is the **first real testable claim** of the entire concept.

If this fails, the larger system is not viable.

## Hypothesis
A structured membrane interface with increased surface area and controlled flow can improve oxygen transfer rate vs. static water.

## Experimental Setup (Benchtop)
- Water tank with controlled dissolved oxygen
- Air or oxygen source
- Membrane interface (synthetic or biomimetic)
- Flow control (pump or circulation)
- Dissolved oxygen sensor (mg/L)

## Variables
- Surface area of membrane
- Flow rate
- Oxygen concentration in input stream
- Temperature

## Measurements
- Dissolved oxygen (mg/L over time)
- Transfer rate (mL O2/min equivalent)
- Efficiency vs baseline

## Success Criteria
- ≥20–30% improvement over baseline diffusion
- Stable operation over repeated trials

## Failure Criteria
- No measurable improvement
- Unstable or inconsistent transfer

## Deliverables
- Experimental data
- Graphs of oxygen concentration vs time
- Repeatability analysis

## Output Value
This milestone creates:
- First real data point
- Credibility for investors
- Basis for next funding step

## Important Constraint
This experiment must remain **non-biological and non-human** at this stage.

## Next Step After Success
Move to fluid systems and compatibility testing.
